Chronologically ordered list of key events:

Patient had transient tightness in chest and shoulder on left side 1 year before presentation.
Patient had miscarriage 1 month before presentation.
Patient experienced acute substernal chest pain that radiated to the jaw and shoulders with dyspnea and belching 5 days before presentation.
Patient experienced substernal chest pain with radiation to the jaw and shoulders that gradually resolved after a few hours on the day before presentation.
Patient presented to the emergency department with substernal chest pain, light-headedness, and dyspnea.
Patient underwent electrocardiography, which showed normal sinus rhythm and submillimeter down-sloping ST-segment depressions in leads V3, V4, and V5.
Patient underwent transthoracic echocardiography, which showed overall normal left ventricular and right ventricular systolic function and a small, focal area of hypokinesis in the left ventricular inferolateral wall.
Patient underwent CT angiography of the chest, which showed no abnormalities.
Patient was administered aspirin, atorvastatin, and intravenous heparin.
Electrocardiography performed 3 hours later showed the ST-segment depressions had decreased in magnitude.
Electrocardiography performed 8 hours later showed the ST-segment depressions had fully resolved.
Patient underwent coronary angiography, which revealed a filling defect in the first obtuse marginal branch of the left circumflex coronary artery.
Patient underwent genetic testing, which was negative.
Six weeks after presentation, patient underwent follow-up treadmill stress testing and reported no chest pain.
Temporal relationships between symptoms and interventions:

Transient tightness in chest and shoulder on left side occurred 1 year before presentation.
Miscarriage occurred 1 month before presentation.
Acute substernal chest pain that radiated to the jaw and shoulders with dyspnea and belching occurred 5 days before presentation.
Substernal chest pain with radiation to the jaw and shoulders occurred on the day before presentation.
Electrocardiography, transthoracic echocardiography, and CT angiography of the chest were performed on the day of presentation.
Aspirin, atorvastatin, and intravenous heparin were administered on the day of presentation.
Coronary angiography was performed after the electrocardiography, transthoracic echocardiography, and CT angiography of the chest were performed on the day of presentation.
Genetic testing was performed after coronary angiography.
Follow-up treadmill stress testing was performed 6 weeks after presentation.
